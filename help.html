Scam Watch

Reporting Scams

Action Fraud is the UK’s national reporting centre for fraud. You can report all types of scams to them, and they’ll get the National Fraud Intelligence Bureau to investigate. They’ll also give you a crime reference number, which can be helpful if you need to tell your bank you’ve been scammed.

You can report scams online at actionfraud.police.uk or by calling 0300 123 2040.

Getting Advice

Citizens Advice offers free and confidential advice on how to deal with scams. You can contact them by calling their consumer helpline on 0808 223 1133 or by chatting online with an adviser.

If you’re helping someone who’s been scammed, Citizens Advice can also provide guidance on how to manage their bank account for them.

Support for Victims

Victim Support provides 24/7 free and confidential support to victims and witnesses of crime in England and Wales. They can be contacted on 0808 168 9111 or through their website.

Think Jessica is a charity that provides emotional and practical help to victims of crimes and scams. They can be contacted through their website.

Mental Health Support

Mind is a charity that provides confidential information and support to people experiencing mental health issues. They have a dedicated support line for people who have been scammed, which can be contacted on 0300 123 3393.
